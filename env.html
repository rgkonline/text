Rationale for Using Un-sanitised Data Bankline uses part sanitised data for testing in Unit, System, User Acceptance and Performance environments. Environments hold data for end to end testing and are shared with other projects, some mandatory, including SEPA Payments. Any sanitisation activity will impact these projects. Data used for Bankline Testing was originally sourced from Production 7 years ago, and personal information was partially sanitised at a basic level including simple characters replacement. However, this sanitisation is not to the level now required by Information Security policy Sanitisation of the data in these Test environments has been considered, in conjunction with the Sanitisation CoE, and estimated may take c. 3 months at a potential cost of 50k sterling, by Offshore resource, per environment. Performing this sanitisation would require the relevant environment to be unavailable for Testing during the duration of the sanitisation process, and this would significantly hinder the ability for delivery of change on Bankline in 2016, and would have impact on delivery plans for cross platform projects who share these environments. Therefore, Bankline seeks approval for continuing to test with part sanitised data in 2016 Bankline will review options to remove the data the end of the testing period post 21st Dec, bearing in mind that the data shared. Bankline will assist in identifying whether opportunities to fully sanitise the data exist without impact on mandatory projects. Discussions around options for sanitisation are currently happening within the Payments TS Environment team and their management line at this time too Approval of the TDRA and acceptance to Bankline continuing to Test with part sanitised data was accepted by the UK Channels PCC on June 26th 2014. Bankline was formally included on PAN PSG Unsanitised Data Operational Risk Issue, 219579, at CORC, i.e. Compliance and Operational Risk Committee on 30th June 2014.
